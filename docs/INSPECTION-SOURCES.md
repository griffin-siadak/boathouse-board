## Consensus points (appear in 3+ sources)

1. **Wash frequently, rinse thoroughly** — mild soap, no abrasives; hull-up to drain.
2. **Clean, don't lubricate** tracks, wheels, oarlocks — lubricant attracts grit and wears plastic. The two exceptions: ball-bearing seat bearings (light machine oil quarterly) and threads (grease when serviced).
3. **Hardware sweep** — nuts/bolts loosen and corrode; check tightness + corrosion, replace suspect fasteners, anti-corrosion compound at stainless/aluminum contact.
4. **Seat wheels wear out** — inspect regularly, replace as a full set (Filippi: annually).
5. **Stretcher bolts loosen with use** — check frequently; heel ties are a safety item.
6. **Compartments must dry out** — open vents/hatches after rowing, closed while rowing.
7. **Wax/polish 1–2× a season** if sun-exposed or dirty water.
8. **Fix damage immediately** — worn moving parts and hull damage compound quickly; cross-keel hull damage is the serious kind.
